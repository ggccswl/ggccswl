Discipline & conduct

Students must follow proctorial rules. Violations may lead to fines, removal from class, or expulsion, as stated in the college prospectus.

Critical rules (fines & prohibition) ▼
  • Mobile phones: not allowed in college/class per Punjab government; violators fined Rs. 1,000.
  • Smoking in classroom, lab, lawns, corridors — prohibited; fine Rs. 500 on the spot.
  • Plucking flowers: prohibited; fine Rs. 200 per offence.
  • Uniform: students without uniform may not enter the college.
Punctuality & attendance ▼
  • Absence without permission for 6 consecutive days (excluding holidays) may result in being struck off the roll unless the Principal is satisfied with the explanation. Re-admission may be possible within 15 days with absence fine and re-admission fee.
  • Re-admission is allowed only twice per session.
  • Students may not leave class or lab without teacher permission or before dismissal.
  • Poor performance, misconduct, or lecture shortage may block forwarding of admission forms to board/university; roll number slips may be withheld.
Classroom conduct & respect ▼
  • Disruptive students may be marked absent unless the teacher accepts an excuse.
  • Serious misbehaviour may lead to removal from class/lab and referral to the Principal, who may impose penalties including teacher recommendations.
  • Show respect to teaching and non-teaching staff and seniors.
Communication & politics ▼
  • Students must not contact higher authorities directly; correspondence routes through the Principal. Breach may result in expulsion.
  • Unionism, political activity, sectarianism, and collective demonstrations are strictly prohibited.
  • Grievances must be lodged in writing to the Principal with supporting documents — not by bypassing proper channel.
Fees & finality ▼
  • All dues paid to the college are non-refundable.
  • Failure to pay dues, fines, or penalties on time may lead to removal from college rolls.
  • Decisions of the college council are final and not challengeable in court as per prospectus.
  • College disciplinary rules cannot be challenged in courts or elsewhere as stated in the prospectus.
